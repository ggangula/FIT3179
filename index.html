Melbourne Housing Market (2016-2018)

Melbourne, Australia's cultural capital and ranked as the world's most liveable city a whopping seven times. Here is a guide on what to expect if you are interesting in investing into Melbourne's housing market.

Which areas are more affordable?

The map shows the range of average prices of a property across Melbourne. Boroondara and Port Phillip local government areas seem to be the most costly whilst Mitchell Shire, Cardinia and Moorabool are the cheapest. This is expected as they are in Greater Melbourne.

Does distance and number of bedrooms influence the cost?

There is a general trend that as the dwelling is further away from the CBD, the price reduces. There also seem to be less number of bedrooms closer into the CBD. Interestingly, Southern Metropolitan doesn't really follow the trend where house prices are high regardless of how far the dwelling is from the CBD.

How has the market changed over time?

The western side of Melbourne has not changed much over time however the eastern and southern parts of Melbourne seem to generally increase suggesting they are much more popular areas to invest in.

Does dwelling type matter?

Understandably, houses are much more costly in comparison to townhouses and units as they are much smaller in land size. There is significantly more units in Southern and Northern Metropolitan, this could either be due to these areas being considerable close to the CBD or the popularity in housing in these areas. Eastern Metropolitan has much less dwellings in comparison to Northern, Western and Southern. This could potentially be caused by the land size being larger in the east.
